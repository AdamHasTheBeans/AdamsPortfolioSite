About Adam

General info

Hello!
My name is Adam Currier and I am a Software/Game Developer from Vermont.
Since I was a kid I've had a keen interest in exploring an eclectic array of activities and pastimes that grab my interest. This usually leads to things like me dabbling in various art mediums (paints, charcoal, digital, 3d), trying out various activities such as learning piano, trying out odd instruments, or playing disc golf (Vermont is great for disc golf)

Education

I went to high school at Montpelier High School (MHS) where I was introduced to the magic of COMPUTER PROGRAMMING. At the time the idea of computer programming was a pretty abstract concept that I only knew about from things like film and tv, but it ended up becoming one of those things that I was interested in, so I decided to explore it further via the Community Connections program at MHS. This lead to me doing a brief stint in the VTRANS IT division (as I'll explain later)

I decided to enroll at Champlain College, located in Burlington VT. I chose Champlain for a couple of reasons. One of which was the game development curriculum that I found to be quite fascinating (I've had an interest in video games for most of my life). The second reason was it's relatively close placement to home. It was close enough that I could easily keep in contact with my family while also being far enough away to give me a level of independence that I have never experienced before.

While at Champlain there was a brief moment where I was struggling to envision myself in the future I was pursuing. What enabled me to overcome this struggle was stepping back and reflecting on the work I had done up to that point and re-defining where it could take me. Ultimately I think it was overcoming this struggle that brought me to where I am today. It helped me solidify my goals and gave me the strength to plow ahead with some newfound pep in my step. I graduated from Champlain College in May of 2021 with a degree in computer science, a specialization in software engineering, and the goal of pursuing a career in software development.

Expiriences

VTrans

Despite how short the time I spent with the IT division in the Agency of transportation was, it left a pretty big impact on the future I would eventually pursue. It was my first time seeing a real world application of what I would eventually end up going to college for. I was able to see their team dynamic, the environment they worked in as well as the range of services and technology that they had to cover. It was also my experiences there that inspired me to apply to various jobs for the Agency of Digital Services, overall it left a lasting impression that this was a place that I could develop the skills I would need in software development (and that was comforting).

Nicom Coatings is a waterproofing company that I worked for over most of my summer breaks while I was in college (as well as a few winter breaks). My time at Nicom was important because it helped me develop skills that I would need for practically any professional work environment. It was also a job that rewarded having a solid work ethic, so I did my best to develop myself as a valuable teammate. It was also a career that was well outside of my wheelhouse, so it helped me develop my learning skills. Almost Every jobsite would require me to develop the skills necessary to complete the job as well as apply them dynamically. This was crucial because all of the work we did would have a meaningful impact on the buildings we worked on.
